Tunneling Through Rectangular Barrier

Rectangular Potential Barrier

A particle of mass $m$ and energy $E$ encounters a one-dimensional potential barrier $V(x)$:

$V(x)=\{\table ∞, x<-a;0,-a<x<0;V_0,0<x<b;0,x>b$

The height of the potential barrier $V_0$ is greater than the energy of the particle $E$. The initial wave function is $Ψ_{\text"in"}(x)$

$Ψ_{\text"in"}(x)=\{\table √{2/a}\sin(π{x+a}/a),-a<x<0;0, x<-a\text", " x>0$

We define unitless quantities:

$\table α= {mV_0a^2}/ℏ^2, β=b/a, ε=E/V_0, τ={ℏt}/{2ma^2}$

In this simulation, $α=100$, $β=1/2$ and $ε=1/2$. $τ$ is displayed below the graph. The particle passes through the barrier by quantum tunneling.

Survival Probability

The survival probability amplitude $C_{\text"in"}(t)$ is defined as:

$C_{\text"in"}(t)=∫↖ {∞}↙{-∞}dx⋅{Ψ^*}_{\text"in"}(x)ψ(x,t)$

The survival probability $|C_{\text"in"}(t)|^2$ approximates the probability of the particle staying in the initial state $Ψ_{\text"in"}(x)$ after time $t$. If the parameters $α,β$ are chosen such that $αβ^2$ is sufficiently large, $|C_{\text"in"}(t)|^2$ can be approximated by exponential decay.
